About Me

Hi, I am Adam Compton, a Principal Security Consultant with more than 25 years of hands-on experience in penetration testing, research and development, and security automation. I specialize in identifying weaknesses in complex environments, building repeatable processes, and helping others grow in the field of information security.

Professional Journey

My career started in the mid-1990s, long before “cybersecurity” was a household word. Over the years, I have worked across industries including government, small independant companies, education, and Fortune 500 enterprises. I am currently employed as a penetration tester at TrustedSec, where I lead engagements that range from network penetration tests to physical and social engineering exercises.

Career Timeline Sumamry

  • 1981: First exposure to a home computer and programming (Basic).
  • 1995: First exposure to InfoSec, starting with network defense and system administration.
  • 2000s: Transition into penetration testing and red teaming, presenting at early security conferences.
  • 2010s: Focus on automation, tool building, and mentoring newcomers to the field.
  • Present: Principal Security Consultant at TrustedSec and founder of HillbillyStorytime, blending Appalachian storytelling with InfoSec lessons.

Skills & Expertise

Speaking Engagements

I’ve been honored to present at major security conferences around the world, including DerbyCon, DEF CON, Black Hat, BSides, and HackCon. My talks often focus on the human side of InfoSec — the mistakes, the learning, and how to keep moving forward.

HillbillyStorytime

HillbillyStorytime is a personal project that blends Appalachian “Jack Tales” storytelling with information security. It is my way of teaching and sharing lessons through stories — sometimes humorous, sometimes painful, but always with a point. The stories are shared on my YouTube channel, at conferences, and here on this site.

Beyond Security

Outside of InfoSec, I enjoy programming, roleplaying games like Dungeons and Dragons, and spending time with my family. I also have a deep interest in Appalachian folklore and culture, which often inspires the creative side of my projects.
